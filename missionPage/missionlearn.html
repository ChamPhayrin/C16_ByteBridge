Where does the technology come from?

Our organization partners with donors from across the world, who provide us with funding and materials. Everything provided by this organization will be completely free to students.

How will students communicate with instructors?

While the courses are taught in-person and online, we have contact information for organization leaders and instructors that will be made available to students. On the first day of the bootcamp, students will be added to slack channels, where they will be able to ask questions and collaborate with their instructors and peers for additional guidance.

Students will also have opportunities to ask instructors questions following the in-person sessions.

What content is included in the bootcamps?

  • These bootcamp resources include e-books on coding and coding languages, video lectures, coding games, projects, and quizzes for students to complete.
  • Video lectures will be provided in addition to a live, in-person lecture. These lectures will be held in the schools, in an after-school program fashion.
  • Each in-person lecture will be conducted by our organization's staff, from 3pm-5pm, twice weekly
  • Schools will be operating on different weekly schedules, and schedules will be provided prior to the start of the bootcamp.
